Meet the Team

Picture caption

Disaster Mapper is a versatile online web application that implementats various API's (such as the Google Maps API) to create a live, crowd-sourced, and interactive map of disaster areas. The project was developed by a team of Santa Clara University first-year students consisting of: Alexander Kennedy, Zak Graber, Matthew Zhang, Arianne Soriano, Carter Duncan, and Teddy Nguyen. The application was developed during the 2018 "Hack for Humanity" Hackathon, a non-stop 24 hour period of brainstorming, discussion, programming, tweaking, and testing. The result is a global platform that can accept user input, in the form of picures of local natural disasters, which is then output into a map that can be viewed by other residents or first responders. Throughout development, the group enjoyed cooperating, learning, and socializing as a team in which each individual brought specialties to the table.
